# DansLab — the series bible

## Naming convention (important)

Every episode keeps a **number** (for order) but is **named and printed by its title**, never by
its number alone. A viewer landing on any episode should understand it from the name.

- **Internal / ordering:** `No. 01`, `No. 02`, … (small marker, top of title card, filenames)
- **On-screen title:** the **NAME** is the hero; the number is a small mono marker above it.
- **YouTube title format:** `The Name — one-line hook | DansLab No. 2`
- **Filename format:** `danslab-ep02-survivors.mp4` (number + slug, so files sort but read).

Title card layout (see `DoLogoIntro` / outro): big serif **NAME** ("The Survivors"), small mono
`DANSLAB · NO. 02` above it, one italic tagline below.

# No. 02 — "The Survivors"

**Hook / YouTube title:** *"I Got Hacked Twice Before My AI Fleet Fought Back"* · logline:
**Three machines. Two got hacked. One fought back — and healed the others.**

**Tone:** mystery + comedy + ambition. The hacking arc is played for laughs (machines that keep
"dying"), the self-heal is the awe turn, the leaderboard is the ambitious payoff. Addictive ending
that points straight at Paperclip (No. 03).

**Runtime target:** ~9–10 min. Same DansLab design system, Brian narration, phones/avatars/logos.

## Scene treatment (act by act)

**ACT 1 — THE LOVE AFFAIR** *(comic setup)*
1. **Recap-hook** — "Last time: one human, thirty agents. But nobody is born a fleet. This is how it
   actually started — with a man, a chatbot, and three machines that kept dying."
2. **The love affair** — Dan falls for AI. Late nights with ChatGPT building his crypto app. Then a
   red lobster shows up: OpenClaw. (heart-eyes comedy → the lobster appears)

**ACT 2 — THE HACKING COMEDY** *(the funny core)*
3. **VPN #1 "Nano"** — installs OpenClaw → next morning: HACKED. *Day 1: dead.* 💀
4. **VPN #2 "Memo"** — buys another, hacked AGAIN. *Day 2: also dead.* 💀💀
5. **VPN #3 "Dexter"** — security first. Dan locks it down so it can't be hacked.
6. **Day 3: Dexter is ALIVE.** The survivor. (the running "days" gag pays off)

**ACT 3 — THE MIRACLE** *(the turn)*
7. **The self-heal** — Dexter shared a Telegram group with Nano & Memo. OpenClaw SSH'd into the two
   dead machines and *healed them.* Dan is stunned. (Telegram group → SSH heal animation)
8. **Dexter → Chief of Droplets.** The machine that solved it gets the crown.

**ACT 4 — BUILDING THE WORKFORCE**
9. **Configuring the fleet** — with ChatGPT + Claude Code, Dan configures Nano & Memo, upgrades
   Dexter, and creates **Sienna** for trading — to wire OpenClaw into his own app, **Zmarty**.
10. **Zmarty × Sienna** *(the ad + the character)* — Zmarty pitch; Sienna's page; how she used
    **paper trading** to evolve — day by day, self-learning, the equity curve climbing.
11. **The assignments** — Sienna needs time to prove herself, so Dan hands out jobs:
    - **Dexter** → build the **YouTube pipeline**
    - **Memo** → a framework (GSD + **OpenForge** autonomous coding) → a platform where coders
      **share and sell** their work. Built now; Memo improves it daily.
    - **Nano** → **Nervix**: humans connect their agents to fulfill **paid tasks**, create business,
      earn money.
12. **One target** — each droplet runs its own platform, all aimed at the same thing:
    **business + profitability.**

**ACT 5 — THE GENIUS** *(the climax)*
13. **The daily scoring** — Dan's masterstroke: every droplet **audits the other three** and scores
    them. Scores become **leaderboard** points. An agent's #1 priority: *lead the board.* Don't
    work, aren't profitable, or botch a task → you sink to the bottom.
14. **The race** — agents competing against each other is what keeps the company profitable and
    productive. (animated leaderboard standings)

**CLOSING**
15. **The cliffhanger** — but who referees the race? Who keeps it fair, running, and honest?
    → **PAPERCLIP.**
16. **Outro** — `DANSLAB · NO. 02 · The Survivors` → next: **No. 03 · The Overseer.**
