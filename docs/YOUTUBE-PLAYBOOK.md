> they treat the intro and the end and the subject — otherwise we work blind."
> This is that research. It feeds CHANNEL-LAW.md (the laws), the viewer gate
> (`engines/viewer_gate.py`, enforcement), and step 1/2 (research + script).
>
> Method: per-creator web research with cited example videos. Every claim is
> tagged **[strong]** (multiple sources or the creator's own words) or
> **[inferred]** (single source / pattern analysis). Compiled 2026-07-22.

Creators covered: MrBeast, Veritasium, Johnny Harris, Lemmino, Fern,
Kurzgesagt, Vox, MrBallen, Wendover Productions, ColdFusion.

## 1. MrBeast

### First 30 seconds
- **0:00–0:05** — one bold spoken sentence that validates the title/thumbnail
  and states stakes. No branding, no greeting, no title card — ever. [strong]
- **0:05–0:15** — visual PROOF the premise is real (sets, cash, crowd), cuts
  every 3–7s. Leaked internal doc: "front-load as much information about the
  video as possible." [strong — leaked "How to Succeed at MrBeast Production"]
- **0:15–0:30** — the game/challenge literally begins. No setup padding. [strong]
- Documented re-hook cadence: planned "re-engagement" beats at ~3:00 and
  ~6:00 ("something highly interesting and impressive"). [strong — leaked doc]
- Example: **"$456,000 Squid Game In Real Life!"** — 0:00: "I've recreated
  every single set from Squid Game in real life, and whichever one of these
  456 people survives the longest, wins 456 grand." Premise, stakes, and game
  one inside ~15 seconds. [strong]
- Example: **"I Spent 50 Hours Buried Alive"** — first frame IS the thumbnail
  moment (Jimmy already in the coffin in the grave). [strong]

### Ending
- **Abrupt cut on the payoff** — winner crowned / money handed over, cut while
  energy is high. No outro, no credits, no spoken next-video pitch; the
  endscreen overlay does the chaining. [strong]
- Subscribe asks happen early/mid-video and are incentive-framed, never parked
  in an outro. [inferred]

### Subject choice
- **Title-first ideation**: "CTR is what dictates what we do for videos.
  'I Spent 50 Hours In My Front Yard' is lame… you would click 'I Spent 50
  Hours In Ketchup.'" and "'I Survived' instead of 'I Spent'" ups CTR. [strong]
- The "Wow Factor": "Anytime we do something that no other creator can do,
  that separates us in their mind." [strong]
- Optimizes exactly three metrics: CTR, AVD, AVP. Title = extreme claim,
  thumbnail = single high-contrast proof image, hook = the same promise spoken
  at 0:00. One promise, three expressions. [strong]

Sources: simonwillison.net/2024/Sep/15 (leaked doc), cybernews.com, dexerto.com,
creatorhandbook.net, typefully.com/LowKeyJude (733-video study),
en.wikipedia.org/wiki/$456,000_Squid_Game_in_Real_Life!, simonowens.substack.com,
lexfridman.com/mrbeast.

## 2. Veritasium

### First 30 seconds
- **0:00–0:10** — cold open on the counterintuitive thesis, spoken as the very
  first sentence. Example: **"Why Gravity is NOT a Force"** opens: "The General
  Theory of Relativity tells us gravity is not a force, gravitational fields
  don't exist." Title promise delivered in ~8 seconds. [strong — transcript]
- **0:10–0:30** — sharpen the confusion: street interviews voicing the
  misconception, or a demo that makes the wrong intuition feel right.
  Example: **"The Biggest Myth In Education"** — real people answer "I'm a
  visual learner" in the first ~30s, then the thesis lands. [strong]
- Method is research-backed: Muller's PhD found presenting misconceptions
  BEFORE the correct explanation produces more learning. His words: "clarity
  numbs the mind, but confusion can crack it open." [strong]
- Re-hooks via nested questions ("but if that's true, why…?"), not clock
  beats. [inferred]

### Ending
- Misconception fully resolved → reflective philosophical coda (Gravity ends
  on Einstein's "happiest thought") → sponsor read at the very end →
  endscreen. No branded outro, no "smash like" language. [inferred — stable
  pattern across catalog]

### Subject choice
- Picks topics where the audience holds a **strong, confident, WRONG
  intuition** — the stronger the prior, the better the video. [strong]
- His own packaging framework ("We Need To Talk About Clickbait"):
  **"Legitbait"** = attention-maximized packaging that DELIVERS, vs
  "Clicktraps" that over-promise. Adopting legitbait packaging grew the
  channel exponentially. [strong — creator's own video]

Sources: scientificamerican.com profile, Muller PhD (researchgate),
transcript.lol (Gravity), veritasium.com/videos (Clickbait, shade balls),
poynter.org analysis.

## 3. Johnny Harris

### First 30 seconds
- **Visual anchor BEFORE context** — his signature inversion of TV journalism:
  open with the most curiosity-inducing footage, explain after. "Experience
  it, then understand it." [strong]
- **0:15–0:60** — big visual anchors "deliver a promise to the audience";
  then the driving mystery question of Act 1. His framing: **"every story is
  a promise"** (How I Write podcast, 2025). [strong — creator's own words]
- Re-hook mechanism for long-form: alternate anchor → context → anchor for
  the whole runtime; script and visuals written simultaneously. [strong]
- Example: **"How The US Stole The Philippines"** — zooming-map cold open
  ("…7,000 islands… inhabited for over 30,000 years") before any thesis. [strong]
- Example: **"The REAL Reason McDonald's Ice Cream Machines Are Always
  Broken"** — opens on the universally-felt anomaly, then the inciting email:
  "that's where it started to get spooky." [strong]

### Ending
- Act 3 = the mystery answered, then a zoom-out to a bigger moral/geopolitical
  takeaway in reflective first-person — a "keeps you thinking" close, soft
  Patreon/subscribe mention folded in. [strong]

### Subject choice
- **Framing beats subject**: breakfast, olive oil, ice cream machines all go
  viral because packaging turns the mundane into a mystery/accusation
  ("The REAL Reason…", "How the US Stole…"). The question is the product. [strong]
- Marketing is step one: title/thumbnail promise is part of the writing
  process, not post-production. ~4-month cycle per video. [strong]

Sources: How I Write podcast (howiwrite.substack.com), storybench.org,
chengweihu.com/io/visual-before-context, patreon.com/posts/script-how-us,
iheart.com Created podcast.

## 4. Lemmino

### First 30 seconds
- **0:00–0:05** — cinematic cold open: archival audio/news-clip montage over
  an animated reconstruction, OR narration opening directly on a precise
  date-and-place sentence. Never a channel intro, never a promise-speech. [strong]
- **By 0:15** — the central figure/event of the mystery is introduced; **by
  0:30–0:60** the core mystery is fully on the table. [strong]
- Example: **"The Search for D.B. Cooper"** — archival news audio ("We don't
  know who he was, where he came from, or where he went") over the animated
  night jump; the original score cuts to an abrupt stop as he falls into the
  forest, then: "In the afternoon of November 24, 1971, a middle-aged man
  carrying a briefcase walked into Portland International Airport…" [strong —
  transcript]
- Example: **"Cicada 3301: An Internet Mystery"** — the first sentence IS the
  hook: "On the 4th of January, 2012, a user on 4chan posted this image…"
  Inside the first cipher within a minute. [strong — transcript]
- All music self-composed; atmosphere does as much hooking as the words.
  Minimal title card lands after the cold-open beat, not at 0:00. [strong /
  timing inferred]

### Ending
- **Payoff = adjudication, not summary**: candidate theories weighed, then he
  argues for the simplest, most mundane explanation — his differentiator among
  mystery channels. [strong]
- Final beat: a short reflective landing on uncertainty itself. D.B. Cooper
  verbatim: "Did Cooper survive? I have no idea. But as long as that
  possibility can not be dismissed, the legend… will no doubt persist." [strong]
- **No sponsor read, no verbal CTA, no next-video chain** — the video ends on
  the reflection; support is Patreon + a published annotated bibliography per
  documentary. [strong for transcribed videos]

### Subject choice
- Stock unsolved mysteries with an internet-native twist (D.B. Cooper, Cicada
  3301, MH370, Dyatlov) + existential-science entries. [strong]
- **The bar is set by cadence**: ~one documentary a year, "when it's done" —
  a topic must sustain months of research, custom animation, an original
  score, and at least one piece of ORIGINAL analysis (Cicada airport-distance
  mapping; titanium particles on Cooper's tie). Depth IS the hook. [strong]
- Titles: plain mystery-forward noun phrases ("The Search for…", "An Internet
  Mystery"); thumbnail: single dark minimalist image; the cold open pays both
  off immediately. [inferred]

Sources: tvtropes.org WebVideo/Lemmino, youtubetotranscript.com (D.B. Cooper),
ytscribe.com (Cicada 3301), youtube.fandom.com Wikitubia interview, lemmi.no.

## 5. Fern

### First 30 seconds
- **0:00–0:05** — hard cold open that "immediately delivers on the thumbnail
  promise": confession footage, mid-action shots, or a precise timestamp
  anchor. "No 'hey guys', no channel intros, no wasted seconds" (10-video
  retention audit, avg hook score 8.5/10). [strong — single-analyst audit]
- **0:05–0:30** — escalating procedural beats build dread BEFORE any context.
- Promise lands ~0:60–2:00 as an explicit question once the cold open
  resolves violently or mysteriously. Every video: mystery → reveal. [strong]
- Example: **"How a Pilot Hijacked a Plane"** — "At 9:27 am on the 24th of
  March 2015, a plane got in contact with the Marseilles enroute control
  center… Everything seemed normal." Then ~80 seconds of nothing but
  radio-silence beats (9:30… 9:34… 9:40 radar lost) ending "…crashed into
  the side of a mountain in the Alps. **What happened?**" — then the
  reveal-and-rewind: "The plane was hijacked, but not by terrorists. Let's
  go back." [strong — full transcript]
- Example: **"Mapping the Trump Shooting"** (21M+, published ~3 days after
  the event) — the thumbnail promise (the 3D map) IS the opening. [strong for
  stats; opening beat inferred]
- Signature visual: Blender 3D spatial reconstruction — the camera flying
  through the reconstructed plane/building. [strong]

### Ending
- **Payoff → human aftermath → systemic question**: Germanwings ends on the
  victims, the widow's foundation, the EASA two-person-cockpit rule — "The
  families have faced losses that no money can heal." [strong — transcript]
- **Epistemic-honesty landing** is a signature: "…a dilemma best left to
  healthcare professionals and ethics professors. And unfortunately we're
  YouTubers. So we encourage you to read more in these articles." [strong]
- Sponsor read APPENDED after the story, radically transparent framing ("it
  was insanely hard to make… that's why we have sponsors"). [strong for this
  video; default placement inferred]
- **Anti-pattern to NOT copy** (from the same audit): a 3–6 minute history
  dump before the main story resumes, in 7/10 videos — "the hook earns 5
  seconds of trust; then 4 minutes of context spends all of it." Flat
  140–160 WPM pacing regardless of emotional register. [strong — quoted audit]

### Subject choice
- "Armchair documentaries, almost weekly" — ~60–100 person team; topics must
  be **reconstructable in Blender within weeks** (the opposite constraint
  from Lemmino). [strong]
- Topic space: modern disasters, hijackings, scams, corporate villainy +
  news-reactive spatial journalism (Trump shooting in days = biggest video). [strong]
- Triangle: active-voice incident titles ("How a Pilot Hijacked a Plane"),
  thumbnail = the 3D reconstruction, cold open = the thumbnail in motion.
  The cold open exists to instantly cash the thumbnail's check. [strong]

Sources: youtubetotranscript.com (Germanwings), Seth Fowler 10-video audit
(LinkedIn), youtube.fandom.com/wiki/Fern, sigmastory.in, dexerto.com,
watchfern.com/about.

## 6. Kurzgesagt

### First 30 seconds
- **0:00–0:15** — spoken thesis-hook in narrator voice, often meta or
  second-person; animation starts immediately. **0:15–0:30** — the turn: the
  stakes are made PERSONAL ("Nuclear weapons are about you"); collab/sponsor
  disclosure here if applicable. **Then** the branded intro jingle (bird/duck
  sequence) — after the hook paragraph, never at 0:00. [strong; exact jingle
  second inferred, typically ~0:20–0:45]
- Example: **"What if We Nuke a City?"** — "Playing around with nuclear
  weapons in videos is fun… There's a visceral joy in blowing things up" —
  a meta-hook indicting their own genre — then "this isn't about silly stacks
  of TNT… Nuclear weapons are about you." [strong — transcript]
- Example: **"The Egg"** — pure narrative cold open at 0:00: "You were on
  your way home when you died." No thesis, no framing. [strong]
- Dettmer (Forbes 2024): "After so many years, I still do not have a good
  grasp of what really works" — even at 20M subs, hooks are tested, not
  known. [strong — creator's own words]

### Ending
- **Reframe → agency → optimistic-nihilist landing → CTA → duck outro.**
  After the darkest point, pivot to what can be done ("There is a solution
  though!… Visit notonukes.org"). [strong — transcript]
- "Optimistic nihilism" is the channel's thesis statement (their 2017 video):
  even apocalypse videos end on meaning and agency. [strong]
- Outro furniture: the recurring duck gag, Patreon thanks, endcards for
  next-video chaining (endcards, not spoken teases). Sponsor reads sit at the
  EDGES (intro disclosure or appended endcard) — never mid-explanation;
  "Kurzgesagt has the last word on the script" (walked from a pharma deal
  over it). [strong for rules; "never mid-roll" inferred]

### Subject choice
- **Existential curiosity with personal stakes**: black holes, nukes, immune
  system, loneliness — hypotheticals ("What if…") convert abstract science
  into first-person scenarios. [strong]
- Research-led gating: papers first, THEN decide if the topic is interesting
  enough; 2–3 outside experts per script; ~1 video/month. Topic selection is
  conviction + rigor, not trend-chasing (their virus videos overperformed
  unexpectedly). [strong — Forbes]
- Triangle: question/bold-declarative titles, single high-contrast
  house-style illustration (the style IS the brand recognition), opening
  lines restate the title's question and personalize it within 15s. [inferred]

Sources: forbes.com Dettmer interview (2024), lingq.com transcript (Nuke a
City), medium.com/@Kurzgesagt (sponsorships), kurzgesagt fandom wiki (intro
history, duck), glasstire.com (optimistic nihilism), galactanet.com (The Egg).

## 7. Vox

### First 30 seconds
- **0:00–0:03** — bold visual: archival clip, map, or "a number that sounds
  impossible." No branding, no greeting. [strong — 50-video pattern analysis]
- **0:03–0:07** — narrator poses the question over the footage (~15–20 words
  spoken before the title card). [strong]
- **0:07–0:12** — hard cut to the title card (the question in Vox editorial
  type). [strong]
- **0:12–0:30** — widen the question with 2–3 archival/graphic beats; answer
  nothing yet. A map appears at 0:50–1:15 in 43 of 50 analyzed videos. [strong]
- Example: **"Why this black hole photo took so long"** (Joss Fong) —
  deliberately does NOT open on the photo; opens on telescope flyovers. Fong:
  "I didn't want the photo to be the first thing that people see" — the famous
  image is withheld as the mid-video payoff. [strong]
- Example: **"Why cartoon characters wear gloves"** — opens directly on
  rubber-hose animation archival; the asset IS the hook. [strong]

### Ending
- Answer the title question → zoom out to implication, **or end on an open
  question** (discussion bait — the 50-video analysis: "the video ends on the
  question, not the answer"). Summary statements signal closure. [strong]
- No spoken CTA; the end card does it silently. [inferred]

### Subject choice
- **"The question you didn't know you had"** — make the trivial nontrivial
  (Phil Edwards). Topic test: is it BETTER in video than text? (Joss Fong:
  "we want the stories that are specifically better in video"). [strong]
- Asset-driven selection: start from a powerful archival/visual asset and
  research outward. Edwards pre-tests titles + thumbnails in a private Discord
  before producing. [strong]
- Thumbnails: diagrammatic mini-infographics (color blocks, labels, arrows),
  not face+shock. [strong]

Sources: medium.com/@seijiyushin (50-video analysis), theopennotebook.com
(Fong black-hole breakdown), theexplainers.substack.com (Edwards),
niemanlab.org, storybench.org (Borders).

## 8. MrBallen

### First 30 seconds — a verbatim machine
- **0:00–0:10** — spoken story tease, no names, no spoilers: "Today's story is
  going to be really, really weird… a lot of what happened has never been
  fully explained." [strong — transcripts]
- **~0:10** — the signature pivot, word-for-word stable for years: "But before
  we get into today's story, if you're a fan of the strange, dark and
  mysterious delivered in story format, you've come to the right channel,
  because that's all we do…" [strong]
- **~0:15–0:35** — the absurdist like/subscribe joke (personifying the Like
  button), ALWAYS inside the intro so the story is never interrupted. [strong]
- **~0:35** — "Okay, let's get into today's story." Zero interruptions until
  the payoff. [strong]
- His craft rule: **"If you want a story to land, you start with the end…
  be absolutely ruthless about not giving it up before the end."** Bans
  telegraphing lines; tension via limited POV. [strong — Modern Wisdom]

### Ending
- The withheld twist lands in the final minute → short factual coda ("bones…
  are still being found on King William Island") → soft brand outro + fixed
  sign-off ("I really appreciate your support. Until next time. See ya.").
  Multi-story episodes chain the next tease straight after each payoff. [strong]

### Subject choice
- The bar: genuinely strange + dark + mysterious **with an un-tippable
  payoff** — no twist, no episode. Victim-oriented, three-dimensional
  characters so the audience invests before tragedy. [strong]
- Titles: curiosity-first, spoiler-free, second-person stakes ("You think
  you're safe at home…"). Thumbnails: his face + one mysterious image + a red
  arrow. Same camera, same hoodie — consistency is the brand. [strong]

Sources: podscripts.co (Masterwork, Modern Wisdom #774), happyscribe
(Frozen Hell), sherisesstudios.com, entreresource.com/mr-ballen.

## 9. Wendover Productions

*(evidence below verified directly against yt-dlp transcripts + frame contact
sheets of the cited videos)*

### First 30 seconds
- **0:00–0:08** — sponsor billboard over the channel logo card BEFORE anything
  else (verified: "The Logistics of the US Election" 0:00–0:06 Dashlane;
  "How Air Ambulances (Don't) Work" 0:00–0:15 CuriosityStream). This is the
  only brand appearance — **no title card for the video itself, ever**;
  the title lives in metadata only. [strong]
- **~0:08–0:25** — hard cut into the narrative, mid-world. No greeting, no
  "in this video". Election: concrete future scene (election day, millions
  heading to polls). Air Ambulances: in-media-res WWII battlefield scene
  (Dr. R Adams Cowley) opening a video that is actually about private-equity
  economics. [strong — transcripts]
- **0:25–0:45** — the signature **stakes-scaling move**: concrete scene →
  systemic scale ("one day of voting" → "multi-year, multi-billion-dollar
  machine"). [strong]
- **0:45–1:00** — first hyper-specific anecdote or map zoom (Election: US map
  → Dixville Notch pin at ~0:50). Faceless narrator; stock footage + maps
  from second one. [strong — frames]

### Ending
- **Two-part close**: the last content line is the thesis/implication — never
  a summary or joke (Election ~8:31: results take weeks, and the clock resets
  on the next four-year cycle; Air Ambulances ~20:45: the system stays broken
  because a PE investor prefers 10.5% returns over 10.4%) → beat of silence →
  hard cut into a 60–90s sponsor/Nebula read to the final second. [strong]
- **Zero engagement bait** — no like/subscribe ask anywhere; the only CTA is
  the sponsor/Nebula funnel. [strong in sampled videos]

### Subject choice
- Formula: **the hidden system behind a mundane or newsworthy thing** — the
  route, the constraint, and the margin. "Here is a system you don't think
  about, and here's why you should." [strong]
- Title corpus (25 recent uploads, July 2026): declarative system-indictments,
  almost never questions — "How Car Dealerships Scam America", "Why American
  Houses Are So Flimsy", "The Problem With Spirit Airlines' Business Model".
  Stems: "How [entity] [verb]" (9/25), "Why [thing] is [state]" (4/25),
  "The [Logistics/Problem/Crisis] of X". [strong — title corpus]
- Curiosity comes from the gap between the mundane subject and the systemic
  claim ("scam", "ruined", "failed") — not from hype language. Thumbnails: one
  clean aerial/stock photo of the system's most photogenic asset, little or
  no text. [inferred]

Sources: yt-dlp transcripts + frames of youtube.com/watch?v=RyG7nzteG64 and
3gdCH1XUIlE, yespress.io/wendover-productions, en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sam_Denby.

## 10. ColdFusion

*(evidence below verified against yt-dlp transcripts + opening-frame contact
sheets of three videos spanning 2019–2026)*

### First 30 seconds — two verified modes, one branded beat
- **Mode 1, cinematic specials** ("Theranos — Silicon Valley's Greatest
  Disaster"): 0:00 black → aerial drone + music, mythic narration at 0:01;
  "ColdFusion Presents" serif card ~0:08; news-anchor audio montage 0:10–1:00;
  stakes by ~1:05 ($9B company, worst disaster); dual driving question ~1:11;
  **the branded "ColdFusion TV" sting + episode title card lands at ~1:15–1:22
  — 75 seconds in, not at 0:00** — then a chapter card and the body. [strong]
- **Mode 2, standard episodes** ("The Rise and Fall of BlackBerry"; "SpaceX
  Went Public"): optional 7s cold open (vox-pops), greeting catchphrase "Hi,
  welcome to another ColdFusion video" at 0:00–0:10, thesis + numeric stakes
  by ~0:26, explicit promise ("let's take a look at the rise and fall of…"),
  **spoken sting "You're watching ColdFusion TV" at ~0:31** with a music-only
  breath (0:33–0:40) before the body. [strong — transcripts]
- Music is load-bearing from second zero — much of it Dagogo's own (produces
  as "Burn Water"), which is why the mood is so controlled. [strong]

### Ending — a four-beat close, near-identical for seven years
1. **Lesson/reflection paragraph** over music, with an explicit title echo
   ("…and that is the rise and fall of BlackBerry"). [strong]
2. **Story-specific engagement question** routed to comments ("did you ever
   own a BlackBerry?"), never generic. [strong]
3. **Soft CTA + cross-plugs**, incl. an emotion-redirecting next-video tease
   (after the bleak Theranos story: hopeful medical-breakthrough videos,
   links below). [strong]
4. **Verbatim sign-off**: "My name is Dagogo, you've been watching
   ColdFusion… cheers guys, have a good one" → 5–30s music-only end card with
   the "ColdFusion — new thinking" audio ident. [strong — three transcripts]
- No sponsor read in any sampled ending — monetization is Patreon/podcast,
  not bookend reads. [inferred — sample of 3]

### Subject choice
- Formula: **corporate/tech mystery told as narrative history** — rise-and-
  fall, fraud, comeback — anchored by ONE jaw-dropping stat. [strong]
- The "Rise and Fall" franchise is an explicit repeatable series (BlackBerry,
  Nokia, Yahoo, Kodak, Motorola); "New Thinking" S1/S2 formalizes the
  tech-history storytelling lane. [strong — playlists]
- Title corpus (30 recent uploads, July 2026): conversational crisis
  sentences with a shocking number — "The Metaverse Only Has 900 Users",
  "Porsche Profits Fall 99%…", "The Windows 11 Crisis", even one-word
  "Yikes." — spoken-register asides over SEO stems. [strong — title corpus]
- Where Wendover sells a hidden *system*, ColdFusion sells a hidden *story*:
  hubris, fraud, collapse, comeback + the promise of a calm, cinematic,
  music-driven telling. [strong]

## THE 10 RULES — ranked by evidence strength

Ranked by how hard the evidence is: rules 1–6 rest on creators' own words,
leaked internal docs, or transcript-verified timings across multiple
channels; rules 7–10 rest on strong cross-channel patterns with fewer
first-person confirmations. Each rule names its enforcement point in OUR
pipeline.

### 1. The title/thumbnail/hook are ONE promise, made three ways — and they come FIRST
Evidence: MrBeast leaked doc ("CTR is what dictates what we do for videos"),
Harris ("every story is a promise", marketing is step one of writing),
Veritasium's legitbait framework, Vox's Discord title pre-testing, Fern's
cold-open-cashes-the-thumbnail audit. 10/10 channels: the packaging is
designed before or during scripting, never after.
→ Enforce: step 1 must emit title+thumbnail+hook as one unit; step 2 script
must open by paying that exact promise. Viewer gate Q1–Q2 check the receipt.

### 2. Zero throat-clearing: no greeting, no roadmap, no logo at 0:00
Evidence: 9/10 channels start mid-world at 0:00 (transcript-verified for
Wendover, ColdFusion specials, Lemmino, Fern, Veritasium, MrBeast; MrBallen's
greeting IS his hook formula, the exception that proves the brand rule).
Branding arrives late and short: Vox title card 0:07–0:12, Kurzgesagt jingle
after the hook (~0:20–0:45), ColdFusion sting at 0:31 (standard) / 1:15
(specials). Matches CHANNEL-LAW Law 2 (title beat under 2s, inside the story).
→ Enforce: render step — no logo/title frame before the hook beat.

### 3. Concrete beats abstract in the first five seconds
Evidence: every verified cold open anchors on something specific — a date
("At 9:27 am on the 24th of March 2015…" — Fern), a scene (MrBeast's coffin
first frame), a claim ("gravity is not a force"), a number (ColdFusion's
$9B), a place (Wendover's Dixville Notch). Vox: "a number that sounds
impossible, or a single dramatic shot." Slow fades and generic establishing
shots appear in NO verified opening.
→ Enforce: viewer gate Q1 ("concrete visual hook by 0:05 — slow fades and
gradients are NOT hooks").

### 4. State the promise explicitly by ~0:15, then withhold the payoff
Evidence: MrBeast speaks the full premise at 0:00 and front-loads proof;
Veritasium's thesis in sentence one; Vox's question spoken by 0:07;
Kurzgesagt's personal turn by 0:30; MrBallen's tease-then-pivot at 0:10;
ColdFusion's promise at 0:26. The complement is deliberate withholding:
MrBallen "start with the end… be absolutely ruthless about not giving it
up"; Vox withheld the black-hole photo entirely.
→ Enforce: step 2 script contract — promise sentence inside the first 40
words; the payoff scene may not appear before the final act. Gate Q2.

### 5. The ending is the thesis landed, never a summary or a fade-out
Evidence: transcript-verified across styles — Wendover's last content line is
the implication ("10.5% over 10.4%"), Lemmino lands on the uncertainty
itself, Fern on human aftermath + systemic question, Kurzgesagt on reframe +
agency, Veritasium on the philosophical coda, MrBeast cuts ON the payoff.
Nobody summarizes. Nobody fades out limply.
→ Enforce: step 2 — final scene must reframe the opening promise. Gate Q4.

### 6. Plan re-hooks on a clock, not on vibes
Evidence: MrBeast's leaked doc documents engineered re-engagement at ~3:00
and ~6:00; Harris alternates anchor→context→anchor as his named retention
mechanism; MrBallen chains story-tease after each payoff; Fern's audited
failure mode (3–6 min context dump = "commitment gap") shows what happens
when you don't.
→ Enforce: step 4 scene manifest — no scene block >90s without a new
question, anchor, or escalation; context dumps capped at 45s.

### 7. Forward hook > "subscribe": chain curiosity, don't beg
Evidence: MrBeast ends abruptly and lets the endscreen chain; ColdFusion
redirects emotion to a named next video; MrBallen tees the next story;
Kurzgesagt chains via endcards; Wendover/Lemmino/Vox never verbally beg.
Where a spoken CTA exists it is either branded ritual (MrBallen's joke,
ColdFusion's sign-off) or incentive-framed (MrBeast). Generic "please
subscribe" appears on ZERO of the ten channels. Matches Law 3.
→ Enforce: step 2/10 — end card carries ONE named forward hook; the word
"subscribe" may only appear inside a designed ritual, never bare. Gate Q4.

### 8. Pick subjects where the audience already holds a charged prior
Evidence: Veritasium (confident WRONG intuition — his PhD-backed method),
Wendover (mundane thing + hidden system), ColdFusion (familiar brand +
collapse story), Vox (question you didn't know you had), MrBallen (safe
place + dark story), Kurzgesagt (existential stakes made personal). The
click is the GAP between what the viewer believes and what the packaging
claims. [strong pattern, mixed first-person evidence]
→ Enforce: step 1 idea contract — every topic must name (a) the viewer's
prior and (b) the claim that violates it.

### 9. Study outliers before writing — packaging is reverse-engineered, not invented
Evidence: MrBeast's whole company optimizes CTR/AVD/AVP against known
winners; Veritasium publicly dissected his own viral outlier; Vox pre-tests
packaging; Fern's biggest video was news-reactive within 72h. Direct match
to Law 6. [strong pattern; only MrBeast/Vox document the mechanism]
→ Enforce: step 1 must include an outlier scan of the topic's niche (what
over-performed, which packaging patterns repeat) before the script is
approved.

### 10. One scenario, human stakes, all the way down
Evidence: Kurzgesagt stays inside one visualizable scenario; Fern anchors
every story in named humans ("names, ages, families — the viewer never
forgets why the story matters"); Wendover opens systems through one doctor,
one town; MrBallen builds three-dimensional victims; Harris rides one
anchor. Abstract montages appear only in the failure cases (our own Palest
Light review). Matches Law 4. [strong pattern; least first-person doctrine]
→ Enforce: step 4 — every scene names whose eyes we're looking through;
viewer gate Q3 catches the drift ("the exact second you'd swipe away").
